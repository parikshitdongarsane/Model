Mortality & Morphology

Two separate models from field research in Nagpur, India, presented side by side: one relating heat-stress indices to all-cause mortality, and an independent one relating urban form to the heat index. The two are not coupled — each is explored on its own.

Scope, Assumptions & Limitations

This interactive tool is derived entirely from the two peer-reviewed publications cited above and reproduces their statistical models for illustrative purposes only. All predictions are subject to the assumptions, methodological constraints, and limitations stated in the respective source articles — including, but not limited to, the use of binned regression, all-cause (rather than cause-specific) mortality data, the exclusion of solar radiation and wind speed from the heat-stress indices, and the omission of individual-level demographic and socio-economic factors. The regression relationships are empirically valid only for the city of Nagpur, Maharashtra, India, and only for summertime conditions over the periods analysed in the original studies. Outputs should not be extrapolated to other locations, seasons, or populations, and do not constitute predictive, clinical, or policy guidance.
